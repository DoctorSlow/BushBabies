BushBabies

Bushbabies are a favorite food of chimps. Chimps fashion make-shift spears out of sticks and use them to stab into holes in trees where bushbabies may be sleeping or resting. After each plunge of the spear they smell or taste the tip to see if they hit a bushbaby treasure.

Besides serving as yummy snacks for hungry chimps, bushbabies are also incredibly cute.

See below for an adorable pic of a bushbaby not being eaten by a chimp.

Bushbaby Facts:

  • They're primates and more specifically prosimians related to the slow loris, tarsiers, and lemurs
  • They're name comes from the fact that they have a shrill cry not unlike a baby
  • They use their large ears to locate prey and can catch flying insects in the air
  • In addition to chimps, other creatures that enjoy eating bushbabies are certain large owls, snakes, and big cats
